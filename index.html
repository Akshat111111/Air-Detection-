What is Air Quality Index?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a measure used to report daily air quality, with higher values indicating greater levels of air pollution and greater health concerns. The AQI ranges from 0 to 500, and is divided into six categories, each corresponding to a different level of health concern and having a specific color. An AQI value of 50 or below represents good air quality, while values over 300 represent hazardous air quality.
